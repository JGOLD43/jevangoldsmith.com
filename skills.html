Foundation

First Principles Thinking

Breaking problems down to their fundamental truths.

Rather than reasoning by analogy or accepting conventional wisdom, I try to deconstruct problems to their most basic elements. What do we actually know to be true? What assumptions are we making? This approach takes longer but often reveals solutions that others miss.

I learned this framework from studying how Elon Musk approaches engineering problems, but I've found it applies equally well to business decisions, personal choices, and understanding complex systems.

How I Apply This

Questioning industry "best practices" before accepting them
Building financial models from ground-up assumptions
Evaluating career decisions based on fundamental goals
Foundation

Deep Research

Going beyond surface-level understanding.

When I become interested in a topic, I don't stop at the first article or summary. I dig into primary sources, read academic papers, find the original thinkers, and try to understand the full context. This habit means I often know more about niche topics than people who work in those fields professionally.

The process itself is enjoyable to me. There's a satisfaction in connecting dots that others haven't seen, in finding that one obscure source that changes how you understand everything else.

How I Apply This

Reading original books instead of summaries
Following citation trails to find foundational papers
Synthesizing insights across different domains
Foundation

Clear Communication

Making complex ideas accessible.

I believe that if you truly understand something, you should be able to explain it simply. Writing forces clarity of thought - you can't hide behind jargon or hand-waving when the words are on the page. This is why I write essays: the process of articulating ideas reveals gaps in my own understanding.

Good communication isn't about sounding smart. It's about being understood. The best explanations meet people where they are and build up from there.

How I Apply This

Writing essays to clarify and share my thinking
Explaining technical concepts to non-technical people
Structuring presentations for maximum clarity
Applied Skills
Applied

Financial Analysis

Understanding what the numbers actually mean.

My commerce degree gave me the technical foundation, but real understanding came from applying these tools to actual decisions. I don't just know how to build a DCF model - I understand why it matters, when it's useful, and when other approaches work better.

Financial analysis is ultimately about making better decisions under uncertainty. The models are tools for thinking, not answers in themselves. The goal is to understand the key drivers and sensitivities, not to produce precise predictions.

How I Apply This

Building valuation models for investment decisions
Analyzing real estate development feasibility
Personal financial planning and wealth building
Applied

Strategic Thinking

Seeing the whole board, not just the next move.

Strategy is about making choices that position you well for the long term, even when the short-term costs are real. It requires understanding competitive dynamics, identifying leverage points, and having the discipline to stay focused on what matters.

I've developed this skill through studying business history, analyzing successful companies, and applying strategic frameworks to my own decisions. The best strategists I've studied all share one trait: they think in systems, not events.

How I Apply This

Long-term career and life planning
Evaluating business opportunities and competitive positions
Making resource allocation decisions
Technical Skills
Technical

Spreadsheet Mastery

Excel as a thinking tool, not just a calculator.

Most people use 10% of what Excel can do. I've invested time in learning advanced formulas, data manipulation techniques, and model architecture because spreadsheets are one of the most powerful thinking tools available. A well-built model forces you to be explicit about your assumptions and logic.

The key insight is that spreadsheets should be readable and auditable. If someone else can't understand your model, it's not a good model - even if the calculations are correct.

How I Apply This

Building complex financial models with proper documentation
Data analysis and visualization for decision-making
Automating repetitive tasks with macros and formulas
Technical

AI-Assisted Work

Multiplying my capabilities with intelligent tools.

I've learned to use AI tools like Claude not as a crutch, but as a thinking partner. The skill isn't in prompting - it's in knowing what questions to ask, how to structure problems, and when AI assistance helps versus when it hinders genuine understanding.

This website, for example, was built with AI assistance. But the ideas, the design decisions, the content - those are mine. AI helped me implement my vision faster than I could have alone. That's the right relationship: human direction, AI acceleration.

How I Apply This

Rapid prototyping and implementation of ideas
Research synthesis and information processing
Learning new skills faster through interactive dialogue

Real Estate Development

Understanding how physical spaces get built.

I'm actively studying how real estate development works - from site acquisition to feasibility analysis to project management. This combines my financial skills with a tangible, physical outcome that creates lasting value.

The appeal is the complexity: development touches finance, design, construction, marketing, and community relations. It's a field where deep knowledge compounds over time and relationships matter enormously.

Current Learning Focus

Development feasibility analysis and pro forma modeling
Understanding zoning, planning, and approval processes
Building relationships in the local development community
