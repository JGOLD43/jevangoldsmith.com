People of History

Research

A deep-dive platform for historical and contemporary figures, linking their works, ideas, influences, and primary sources.

This is a research-system experiment. The bet is that people become easier to understand when their ideas, work, influences, contradictions, and primary sources are shown together.

Current question

What is the smallest profile format that teaches how someone thought without becoming a giant biography?

What it is
A research concept for mapping people, ideas, works, influences, and source material.
Why I made it
I wanted a better way to study how important people actually thought, not just read disconnected summaries.
What I learned
The hard part is not collecting more facts; it is designing relationships between them.
Next step
Define the smallest useful profile format and test it with one historical figure.
Prototype shape

Person, works, ideas, influences, contradictions, primary sources, and second-order effects.

Risk

It can become a fact collection instead of a thinking tool.

Openclaw Agents

AI

Building AI agents that automate complex workflows and tasks.

This is where I test which agent workflows survive contact with real work. The interesting part is not that an agent can act. It is whether the loop stays visible, bounded, and useful.

Current question

Where do agents save judgment work, and where do they just create a more confident mess?

What it is
A set of experiments with AI agents that can help run practical workflows.
Why I made it
I want to understand where agents are genuinely useful and where they just add complexity.
What I learned
Agents need tight scopes, visible feedback, and clear handoff points to stay useful.
Next step
Document the best agent workflows and turn repeatable patterns into examples.
Useful pattern

Agents work better when the task has a clear done state, visible logs, and a human review point.

Anti-pattern

Long autonomous loops with vague goals create impressive motion and weak accountability.

Development AI

AI

AI-powered development tools and workflows for accelerating software development and automating coding tasks.

This project tracks how I actually use AI while building: where it speeds me up, where it makes me lazy, and where the human taste layer still matters most.

Current question

What parts of building should be delegated, and what parts should stay stubbornly human?

What it is
A working set of AI-assisted development workflows for research, coding, review, and QA.
Why I made it
I want to build faster without outsourcing taste, judgment, or responsibility.
What I learned
The best AI workflow is still a human workflow with better feedback loops.
Next step
Write up the strongest patterns and connect them to the technical skills archive.
Working loop

Research, plan, implement, review, QA, then tighten the product judgment.

Standard

Speed only counts if the final thing is clearer, more useful, and easier to maintain.
